What is the bulk modulus?
The bulk modulus is a measurement of the ability of a substance to withstand changes in volume when under compression on all sieds.
How to calculate bulk modulus?
To calculate bulk modulus you should use the following formula:
k = -v * (dp/dv)
where:
k = bulk modulus
p = pressure
v = initial volume of the substance
Why use bulk modulus?
The bulk modulus is sued in order to predict the compression, calculate the density and indirectly indicate the types of chemical bonding within a substance.
